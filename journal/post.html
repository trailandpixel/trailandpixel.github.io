Three days in the Dolomites

Italy · Winter 2025

Rain was falling when I arrived. The mountains were hidden, almost erased behind a wall of fog. It felt like the landscape was holding back its presence.

I walked without expectation. No views, no horizons—just sound, texture, and cold air. The mountains were there, even if I could not see them yet.

The next morning changed everything. Light slowly broke through the valley, revealing shapes that had been hidden the day before.

It was not just a view. It was a shift in atmosphere—like the place was slowly revealing itself, layer by layer.

Sometimes travel is not about where you go, but about what gradually becomes visible.

I left after three days with wet shoes, cold hands, and a memory that felt much larger than the journey itself.
